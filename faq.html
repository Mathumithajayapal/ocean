    frequently asked questions

    Got questions? We have answers.

    Everything you need to know about our marine conservation, cleanup expeditions, and how you can get involved.

    What is Oceanix and what do you do?
    Oceanix is a science-driven marine conservation initiative focused on plastic recovery, coral restoration, and ocean intelligence. We combine advanced cleanup technologies, AI monitoring, and community action to restore ocean health.
    How can I participate in cleanup expeditions?
    You can join our crew by signing up through the “Join the Crew” button. We offer both land‑based coastal cleanups and offshore expeditions. Training, equipment, and safety briefings are provided for all volunteers.
    What technology do you use for plastic recovery?
    We deploy a combination of floating barrier systems, autonomous surface vessels, and AI‑powered debris mapping. Our systems are designed to capture microplastics and macro‑plastics while minimizing bycatch.
    How does coral restoration work?
    We cultivate resilient coral species in nurseries and transplant them to degraded reef areas. Using AI‑powered monitoring, we track growth, health, and biodiversity recovery to ensure long‑term success.
    Is Oceanix a non‑profit or for‑profit organization?
    Oceanix is a mission‑driven social enterprise. We reinvest revenue from technology licensing and partnerships back into conservation programs. Transparency and impact are at our core.
    How do I stay updated on Oceanix projects?
    Subscribe to our newsletter, follow us on social media, or check the “Blog” section. We publish quarterly impact reports and real‑time expedition updates.
