    Expedition Impact

    stories from our Expedition Impact Program to learn more about how Oceanic Society travelers are supporting ocean conservation on the ground.

    Baja California

    Microplastics Monitoring Network

    Travelers collect water samples along shipping routes, contributing to the first open‑source global map of microplastic pollution in the open ocean.

    Read More
    Belize

    Ghost Gear Recovery with Drones

    Using thermal drones and satellite imagery, our teams locate and retrieve abandoned fishing nets, preventing entanglement and reef damage.

    Read More
    Galápagos

    Plastic‑Free Islands: A Zero‑Waste Pilot

    In partnership with local fishermen, we replaced single‑use packaging with reusable containers and eliminated 12 tons of plastic waste from remote island communities.

    Read More
    Mexico

    Mangrove Guardians: Blue Carbon & Community

    Travelers join local women to map, protect, and restore mangroves, creating carbon sinks while supporting sustainable fisheries and ecotourism.

    Read More
    Fiji

    Shark Count: Science Meets Tourism

    Using underwater surveys and photo‑identification, citizen scientists help monitor reef shark populations, providing critical data for marine protected areas.

    Read More
    Costa Rica

    Sea Turtle Rescue & Hatchery Expansion

    Volunteers protect nesting beaches, relocate eggs to secure hatcheries, and release thousands of hatchlings each season — boosting local turtle populations.
